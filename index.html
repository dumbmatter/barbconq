    Barbarian Conquest - Instructions

    Your goal is to find and capture the barbarian city. You lose if all your units are killed by barbarians.

    There are six types of units in the game:

    Scout
    Recon Unit

    Strength: 1
    Movement: 2

    Scouts can't attack.
    Warrior
    Melee Unit

    Strength: 2
    Movement: 1
    Archer
    Archery Unit

    Strength: 3
    Movement: 1

    Archers get bonuses defending hills and cities.
    Chariot
    Mounted Unit

    Strength: 4
    Movement: 2

    Chariots get bonuses attacking axemen.
    Spearman
    Melee Unit

    Strength: 4
    Movement: 1

    Spearmen get bonuses against chariots.
    Axeman
    Melee Unit

    Strength: 5
    Movement: 1

    Axemen get bonuses against warriors and spearmen.

    In addition to the strengths and weaknesses of the units listed above, there are also bonuses depending on the terrain: hills and forests provide defensive bonuses.

    Controls

    Each turn, every one of your units can move. Move the active unit by right clicking with your mouse or by using the numpad on your keyboard.

    To fight a barbarian unit, move onto its tile (or wait for it to move onto yours).

    There are a number of other keyboard shortcuts, mostly copied from Civilization 4.

    Map

    • Arrow keys: pan map
    • C: center screen on the active unit

    Unit Actions

    • Numpad: move active unit any direction (N/NE/E/SE/S/SW/W/NW)
    • G or Right Click: enter "go to" mode for the active unit
    • F: fortify/wake active unit
    • H: fortify active unit until it's healed
    • Space Bar: skip active unit's movement this turn

    Unit Grouping

    By clicking on the unit icons at the bottom of the screen, you can create and modify groups of units that all move together.

    • Shift+Click on unit icon: add to or remove from group
    • Ctrl+Click on unit icon: select all units of same type (use Shift too to add them to a group)
    • Alt+Click on unit icon: select all units (use Shift too to add them to a group)

    Turn

    • Enter: end turn, only after all units have moved
    • Shift+Enter: end turn early

    Strategy

    You can stack your units in the same tile and move them together. This provides better defense, since when a stack is attacked, the best defender against the attacking unit is chosen for battle.

    But if you keep your units together all the time, you won't be able to explore the island and find the barbarian capital quickly. And the barbarians are constantly training new units to fight you, especially in areas out of sight of your troops. The longer it takes you to find the capital, the better defenses it will have.

    Barbarian Conquest - About

    Barbarian Conquest is a free game built on a browser-based reimplementation of the combat system of Civilization 4 (a.k.a. The Greatest Video Game of All Time). I have this completely crazy idea of completely reimplementing Civ4 in a similar manner. If you're interested in helping, please email me.

    Barbarian Conquest is open source! Get the code on GitHub. Contributions are very welcome!

    I want to thank the following open source projects that Barbarian Conquest relies on:

    • TypeScript for automatically finding bugs before I even notice them
    • EasyStar.js for a very easy-to-use pathfinding library
    • howler.js for playing sound effects in any browser
    • Game-icons.net for the unit icons, made by Lorc
    • mistorcaveman for the sound that plays when a battle starts
    • Firefox and Chromium for pushing the web forward

    And finally, if you like basketball, check out my other video game Basketball GM. It is also completely free to play from your web browser.
