Barbarian Conquest - Instructions

Your goal is to find and capture the barbarian city. You lose if all your units are killed by barbarians.

There are six types of units in the game:

Scout

Strength: 1
Movement: 2

Scouts can't attack.

Warrior

Strength: 2
Movement: 1

Archer

Strength: 3
Movement: 1

Archers get bonuses defending hills and cities.

Chariot

Strength: 4
Movement: 2

Chariots get bonuses attacking axemen.

Spearman

Strength: 4
Movement: 1

Spearmen get bonuses against chariots.

Axeman

Strength: 5
Movement: 1

Axemen get bonuses against warriors and spearmen.

In addition to the strengths and weaknesses of the units listed above, there are also bonuses depending on the terrain: hills and forests provide defensive bonuses.

Each turn, every one of your units can move. Move the active unit by right clicking with your mouse or by using the numpad on your keyboard.

To fight a barbarian unit, move onto its tile (or wait for it to move onto yours).

Strategy

You can stack your units in the same tile and move them together. This provides better defense, since when a stack is attacked, the best defender against the attacking unit is chosen for battle. But if you keep your units together all the time, you won't be able to explore the island and find the barbarian capital quickly. And the barbarians are constantly training new units to fight you, especially in areas out of sight of your troops. The longer it takes you to find the capital, the better defenses it will have.

Barbarian Conquest - About

Barbarian Conquest is a free game built on a browser-based reimplementation of the combat system of Civilization 4 (a.k.a. The Greatest Video Game of All Time). I have this completely crazy idea of completely reimplementing Civ4 in a similar manner. If you're interested in helping, please email me. I'm particularly in need of help from people with any of the following skills:

  • Deep understanding of Civ4
  • Experience with video game AI
  • Design/artwork talent

I want to thank the following open source projects that Barbarian Conquest relies on (in no particular order):

  • TypeScript for the fantasting programming language
  • EasyStar.js for a very easy-to-use pathfinding library
  • Firefox and Chromium for pushing the web forward

And finally, I want to thank Lorc and everyone involved with Game-icons.net for providing the free icons I used for units.
